5. Enterprise-Grade Security

Security is not a feature at Acme; it is the foundation upon which our platform is built. We employ a defense-in-depth strategy, layering multiple physical, technical, and administrative controls to protect customer data. Our security program is managed by a dedicated team under the direction of our Chief Security Officer (CSO).

5.1 Data Encryption

We ensure the confidentiality and integrity of your data through robust encryption at every stage.
- Data in Transit: All data transmitted between Acme clients (web, desktop, mobile) and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.3 with strong cipher suites. This protects against eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle attacks.
- Data at Rest: All customer data, including messages, files, and database entries, is encrypted at rest on our servers using industry-standard AES-256 encryption. Encryption keys are managed using hardened, regularly rotated key management systems (KMS).
- Enterprise Key Management (EKM): For maximum control, enterprise customers can use their own encryption keys, hosted in their own AWS KMS instance. This gives you auditable control over the keys that encrypt your data.

5.2 Identity and Access Management (IAM)

- Single Sign-On (SSO): Integrate with your existing identity provider (e.g., Okta, Azure AD, Ping Identity) via SAML 2.0 or OpenID Connect for centralized and secure user authentication.
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enforce MFA for all users to add a critical layer of security beyond passwords.
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Assign granular permissions to users and administrators. Define custom roles to control who can create channels, manage users, access compliance tools, or configure integrations.
- SCIM Provisioning: Automate user lifecycle management. Automatically create, update, and deactivate user accounts in Acme as they are changed in your central identity provider.

5.3 Network and Infrastructure Security

- Virtual Private Cloud (VPC): The Acme production environment is hosted within a logically isolated VPC on Amazon Web Services (AWS). Network access control lists (ACLs) and security groups are used to strictly control inbound and outbound traffic.
- Web Application Firewall (WAF): We deploy a WAF to protect against common web application attacks such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS).
- Intrusion Detection and Prevention Systems (IDS/IPS): Our networks are monitored 24/7 by advanced IDS/IPS to detect and block malicious activity in real-time.
- DDoS Mitigation: We partner with leading providers to ensure our services are protected against large-scale Distributed Denial of Service attacks.

5.4 Application Security & DevSecOps

- Secure Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC): Security is integrated into every phase of our development process, from design and threat modeling to code review and automated security testing (SAST/DAST).
- Third-Party Penetration Testing: We engage independent, CREST-certified security firms to conduct annual penetration tests of our platform and infrastructure. Summaries of these reports are available to enterprise customers upon request.
- Vulnerability Management: We have a formal vulnerability management program that includes regular scanning, risk assessment, and a commitment to patching critical vulnerabilities within defined SLAs.
- Bug Bounty Program: We run a private bug bounty program to leverage the global security research community to identify and report potential vulnerabilities.

5.5 Physical Security of Data Centers

Acme leverages the world-class physical security of our cloud provider, AWS. Their data centers are highly secure facilities with multiple layers of protection, including:
- 24/7 security staff and video surveillance.
- Biometric access controls.
- Redundant power, cooling, and networking.
- Regular audits and certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 1/2/3).

Access to these facilities is strictly limited to authorized personnel, and our infrastructure is deployed in a way that ensures no single point of failure.

7. Reliability & Disaster Recovery

Acme is architected for enterprise-grade resilience and availability, ensuring that your organization's communication hub is always online. We commit to a 99.99% uptime SLA for our Enterprise plan customers.

7.1 High-Availability Architecture

- Multi-Availability Zone (AZ) Deployment: Our core services are deployed across multiple, physically distinct Availability Zones within a single geographic region. If one AZ fails, traffic is automatically and seamlessly rerouted to the healthy AZs with no user impact.
- Redundancy at Every Layer: We employ redundancy for all critical components, including application servers, databases, load balancers, and caching layers.
- Automated Health Checks & Failover: Automated systems constantly monitor the health of all services and trigger failover procedures without manual intervention.

7.2 Backup and Recovery Strategy

We have a comprehensive backup strategy to protect against data loss.
- Continuous Data Protection: Production databases are backed up continuously, allowing for point-in-time recovery.
- Immutable Backups: All backups are stored in a separate, access-controlled environment and are immutable, meaning they cannot be altered or deleted for a defined retention period.
- Recovery Point Objective (RPO): Our target RPO is 15 minutes. This means in the event of a major failure, no more than 15 minutes of data would be lost.
- Recovery Time Objective (RTO): Our target RTO is 4 hours. This is the maximum time it would take to restore service in the event of a complete regional disaster.

7.3 Disaster Recovery (DR) Plan and Testing

Acme maintains a formal Disaster Recovery plan that details the procedures for recovering service in the event of a catastrophic failure of an entire geographic region.
- Multi-Region DR: While our primary infrastructure is multi-AZ, we maintain a warm standby DR site in a separate geographic region. Customer data is asynchronously replicated to this DR site.
- Annual DR Testing: The entire DR plan is tested at least annually. This test involves a full failover of production services to the DR site to validate our procedures, tooling, and RTO/RPO targets. A summary of the DR test results can be made available to enterprise customers.
- Incident Response Team: We have a dedicated, 24/7 on-call incident response team trained to manage and resolve service disruptions.

12. Advanced Security & Threat Management

Beyond the foundational security controls outlined previously, Acme employs a proactive and modern security posture designed to address the evolving threat landscape.

12.1 Zero Trust Architecture Philosophy

Acme's infrastructure and application architecture are guided by a Zero Trust philosophy. This means we do not automatically trust any user or service, whether internal or external to our network. Every access request is authenticated, authorized, and encrypted before being granted.

Key principles of our Zero Trust implementation include:
- Identity as the Perimeter: Access controls are based on verified user and device identity, not network location.
- Least Privilege Access: Services and employees are granted the absolute minimum level of access required to perform their function.
- Micro-segmentation: Our production network is segmented into smaller, isolated zones. A compromise in one zone is contained and cannot easily spread to others.
- Continuous Verification: We continuously monitor and validate user and device state, revoking access if anomalous behavior is detected.

12.2 Proactive Threat Intelligence Program

Our security team operates a proactive threat intelligence program to anticipate and defend against emerging threats. This program leverages multiple sources:
- Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT): Monitoring of public forums and security research communities.
- Commercial Threat Feeds: Subscriptions to leading cybersecurity intelligence providers for indicators of compromise (IoCs) and threat actor tactics.
- Information Sharing and Analysis Centers (ISACs): Participation in industry-specific ISACs to share and receive threat information.

Intelligence gathered is used to enrich our security monitoring, create new detection rules in our SIEM, and inform our defensive strategies.

12.3 Incident Response Lifecycle

Acme maintains a formal, documented Incident Response (IR) plan that is tested regularly through tabletop exercises and simulations. Our 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) follows a standardized IR lifecycle based on the NIST framework:

- Preparation: Maintaining trained personnel, up-to-date playbooks, and necessary security tooling.
- Detection & Analysis: 24/7 monitoring of logs and alerts from our SIEM, IDS/IPS, and other security tools to identify and validate potential incidents.
- Containment: Isolating affected systems from the production network to prevent further damage or data exfiltration.
- Eradication: Identifying and eliminating the root cause of the incident, such as patching a vulnerability or removing malware.
- Recovery: Safely restoring affected systems to production and verifying normal operation.
- Post-Incident Activity: Conducting a root cause analysis (RCA), documenting lessons learned, and implementing improvements to prevent recurrence.

Enterprise customers will be notified of any security incident impacting their data in accordance with our contractual obligations and applicable regulations.

12.4 Software Supply Chain Security (SBOM)

We recognize that securing our own code is not enough. Acme has a robust program to secure our software supply chain:
- Dependency Scanning: All third-party libraries and dependencies are automatically scanned for known vulnerabilities (CVEs) as part of our CI/CD pipeline. Builds will fail if critical vulnerabilities are detected.
- Software Bill of Materials (SBOM): We maintain a dynamic SBOM for our platform. This provides a complete inventory of all software components, enabling rapid impact analysis and response in the event of a new vulnerability disclosure (e.g., Log4j).
- Vendor Security Reviews: All third-party sub-processors and critical software vendors undergo a rigorous security and compliance review before being onboarded.

13. Advanced Data Governance & Control

Acme provides enterprise administrators with powerful, granular tools to enforce their specific corporate and regulatory data handling policies.

13.1 Customizable Data Retention Policies

Beyond simple message archives, Acme allows for the creation of highly specific data retention policies to meet legal, regulatory, and business requirements.
- Global Policy: Set a default retention policy for the entire organization (e.g., "retain all data for 7 years").
- Granular Policies: Define custom policies for specific channels or user groups. For example, a channel dedicated to a sensitive M&A project can have its data set to be permanently deleted 30 days after the project concludes, while data in an HR-related channel can be retained for a longer, specific period.
- Policy Actions: Policies can be configured to either: Retain data: Keep data for a specified period, overriding user deletion attempts. Delete data: Automatically and permanently delete data after a specified period.

All retention policy configurations and actions are fully auditable.

13.2 Ethical Walls & Information Barriers

For organizations in finance, legal, and other regulated industries, Acme provides an Information Barriers feature (also known as Ethical Walls). This allows compliance administrators to prevent specific groups of users from communicating with each other when a conflict of interest exists.

When an Information Barrier policy is active between two groups (e.g., an M&A advisory team and a trading desk):
- Users in one group cannot initiate a Direct Message with users in the other group.
- Users cannot be added to the same private channel.
- Users from one group will not see users from the other group in search results or @mentions.

This feature is critical for preventing insider trading and adhering to regulations like FINRA Rule 2241.

13.3 Comprehensive Audit Logs for SIEM Integration

Acme provides detailed, human-readable audit logs that capture a wide range of activities within your enterprise instance. These logs are essential for security investigations, compliance audits, and internal monitoring.

Key Logged Events Include:
- User Activity: User login success/failure, password changes, MFA enrollment, profile changes.
- Administrative Actions: Changes to user roles/permissions, security settings (e.g., SSO configuration), compliance settings (e.g., retention policy changes), and integration approvals.
- Channel & File Activity: Channel creation/deletion/archiving, user joins/leaves, file uploads/downloads, changes to public/private status.
- Access & Export: Activity from eDiscovery tools, legal hold actions, and data exports.

Integration and Access:
- Audit Log API: All audit events are accessible via a dedicated, secure API.
- SIEM Integration: We provide pre-built connectors and support standardized formats (e.g., CEF, LEEF) for seamless integration with major Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) platforms like Splunk, QRadar, and Microsoft Sentinel. This allows your security team to correlate Acme activity with data from other systems in a single pane of glass.

17.3 Service Level Agreement (SLA) Breakdown

The 99.99% uptime SLA for our Enterprise plan is contractually guaranteed and clearly defined.
- Definition of "Uptime": The percentage of total minutes in a calendar month that the core messaging, file sharing, and search services of the Acme platform are available.
- Exclusions: The SLA does not apply to downtime resulting from: Scheduled maintenance (with at least 48 hours prior notice). Failures of third-party integrations or customer-side networks. Force majeure events beyond our reasonable control.
- Service Credits: In the event we do not meet the 99.99% uptime commitment, customers are eligible for service credits applied to their next billing cycle, calculated as a percentage of their monthly fee, scaling with the duration of the downtime. A detailed credit table is provided in our Master Subscription Agreement (MSA).
